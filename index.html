Spooky Halloween Facts

Did You Know?

  • Celtic Origins: Halloween comes from the ancient Celtic festival of Samhain, where people believed the boundary between the living and the dead blurred.
  • All Hallows' Eve: The name "Halloween" comes from "All Hallows' Eve," the night before All Saints' Day.
  • Trick-or-Treating: The tradition of trick-or-treating comes from medieval practices like "souling" and "guising."
  • Jack-o'-Lantern Origins: The first jack-o'-lanterns were made from turnips, based on an Irish legend about Stingy Jack.
  • Costume Tradition: People originally wore costumes to disguise themselves from evil spirits during Samhain.
  • Bats and Spiders: Bats and spiders are associated with Halloween because of their connections to witchcraft in folklore.
  • The Color Scheme: Orange symbolizes the harvest, while black represents death and the dark of winter.
  • Candy Sales: Halloween is one of the largest candy-selling holidays in the U.S.
  • Haunted Houses: Haunted houses became popular in the 1970s as a safer alternative to pranks.
